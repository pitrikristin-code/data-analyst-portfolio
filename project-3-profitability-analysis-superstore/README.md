# Executive Profitability & Discount Impact Analysis
## Business Context
Revenue growth does not always translate into profit growth.
This project analyzes how discounts, product mix, and regional performance impact overall profitability.
________________________________________
## Core Business Questions
•	Is revenue growth translating into profit growth?
•	Which product categories and sub-categories are eroding profit?
•	How does discount level impact profit margin?
•	Are certain states structurally unprofitable?
•	What actions should management take to improve profitability?
________________________________________
## Dataset
•	Superstore Sales Dataset (Kaggle)
•	Period: 2014–2017
•	Currency: USD
________________________________________
## Analytical Approach
•	Cleaned and transformed transactional sales data using SQL
•	Built analytical tables focusing on revenue, profit, margin, discount, and geography
•	Designed an executive-level Tableau dashboard for decision-making
________________________________________
## Key Insights
•	Revenue growth does not consistently translate into profit growth.
•	Discounts above 50% consistently generate negative profit margins.
•	Office Supplies contributes high revenue but drives significant losses.
•	Several states show structural unprofitability due to aggressive discounting.
________________________________________
## Business Recommendations
•	Implement discount caps, especially for low-margin categories.
•	Rebalance product mix toward higher-margin products.
•	Apply region-specific pricing strategies.
•	Reduce promotional activities in consistently unprofitable regions.
